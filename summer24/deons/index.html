The Black Panther Party

Oakland, California, 1966: A powerful force emerged that would shake the foundations of American society - the Black Panther Party. While often associated with its male leaders, the revolutionary sisters carried the movement on their backs.

Women played a crucial and frequently overlooked role, making up a significant portion of the Party's membership, estimated at up to two-thirds in some chapters. These bold and brilliant Black women, their afros a proud symbol of their culture, were involved in all aspects of the Party's activities. From the frontlines of protests to community kitchens, from political education to leadership roles, Panther women fought tirelessly for racial justice and liberation. Their contributions ranged from implementing vital community service programs to shaping the Party's ideology and direction.
